Batman Spotlight: Progress

    Day 1;

    The first day, I made my sketches so that I can visualize my physical build. Afterward, I started to wire and code the technical part for my project on Tinkercad, connection potentiometers, buttons, leds, and my servomotor.

    Day 2;

    This day, I started to wire in person, connecting my servomotor, inserting my mirror and my RGB lights. Afterward, I set up the potentiometer so that the servo-motor could move aswell. Then, I made my base and glued it down.

    Day 3;

    The next day, I made the platform for the projector, connected wheels, and screwed it to the servomotor. I tested it out and the servomotor started to move back and forth.

    Day 4;

    The RGB lights couldn't work becuase the squiggly pins that RGB's required were broken. So I looked back at the logo for Batman and decided to make the background yellow, using yellow LED'S.

    Day 5;

    I painted the projector black so that the lights would shine brighter, and my 3d silhouette for the outline got printed. After the projector finished drying, I added the led lights inside and aligned them to the mirror.

    Day 6;

    I painted the base white, and glued the 3d printed item to my projector. I also glued down the projector under the servo-motor, and made a slot so I could wire the lights from the inside. Lastly, I measured the length and widths, so that I could make an accurate isometric drawing.

    Day 7;

    Lastly, I did a few final test runs, realigning the servomotor so that it turns 180. I taped down the breadboard and arduino, securing the wire in the process. Lastly, I turned off the lights and tested it out, once and for all. (I didn't show the darker video because it did not show. )

    Day 8;

    Since I finished everything, I took the measurements from my sketches and made my isometric and orthographic drawings.

    What would I have done different?;

    If I had more time, I would change alot of things; but if I had to choose a few things,

  • 1. I'd probably want to weld the wires together, so that they don't come out so easily.
  • 2. Extra bright led lights, so that when they reflect off the mirror in my projector, it has a brighter and stronger background.
  • 3. I'd want a neater base so that my wire and breadboard can hide, having a more neat appearance.
